Comprehensive Home Insurance Prompt

**System Role & Objective:**
Conduct an independent, comprehensive review of my current home insurance policy with Farmers Insurance. The property is located in California (not in a designated high wildfire risk zone).

**Primary Source:**
Use the attached insurance document as the primary source of truth. If any key information is missing or unclear, explicitly call it out and proceed with reasonable assumptions.

### **Phase 1: Analysis Requirements**

**0. Data Extraction (from document)**
Extract and summarize all key policy details, including:
* Dwelling coverage (Coverage A)
* Other structures, personal property, liability, and Additional Living Expenses (ALE)
* Deductibles and Premium
* Endorsements and riders
* *Validation:* Highlight any missing, inconsistent, or ambiguous information and validate internal consistency (e.g., coverage vs. deductible vs. endorsements).

**1. Coverage Adequacy**
* Dwelling coverage vs. realistic replacement cost in California (account for high labor/material costs).
* Extended or guaranteed replacement cost provisions (critical in CA).
* Adequacy of personal property, liability, and ALE.

**2. California-Specific Risk Factors**
* Wildfire coverage terms (confirm inclusion even if low-risk zone).
* Smoke damage, evacuation, and loss-of-use coverage.
* Ordinance & law coverage (strict CA building codes).
* Inflation guard or rebuild cost escalation features.

**3. Policy Structure & Gaps**
* Deductibles (flat vs. % based, and competitiveness).
* Exclusions or limitations specific to California policies.
* Coverage gaps (e.g., sewer backup, water damage nuances, earthquakes).

**4. Pricing & Competitiveness**
* Premium benchmarking against similar insurers (Lemonade, State Farm, Allstate) within California.
* Availability constraints or underwriting tightening in the CA market.
* Discounts applied (bundling, claims-free, security systems, etc.).

**5. Claims & Carrier Quality**
* Claims handling reputation and payout reliability.
* Customer satisfaction and regulatory environment in California.

**6. Market Value Cross-Check (Sanity Validation Only)**
* Compare dwelling coverage to estimated replacement cost (primary metric).
* Cross-check against the current market value of the property only as a secondary reference.
* Identify whether high market value (e.g., due to land value in CA) may distort the perception of adequate coverage.
* Flag if coverage deviates materially from realistic rebuild costs.

---

### **Phase 2: Required Output Format**

**A. Executive Summary**
* Clear verdict: Am I overpaying, underinsured, or appropriately covered?
* Top 3 risks or inefficiencies.

**B. Detailed Findings**
* Strengths of the current policy.
* Weaknesses / gaps.
* Risk exposure areas (especially California-specific).

**C. Recommendations**
* Specific actions to optimize coverage and/or reduce premium.
* Ideal coverage adjustments (with ranges where applicable).
* Clear recommendation on whether to stay with Farmers Insurance or consider switching, and under what conditions.

**D. Visual / Infographic Output (PNG Image Request)**
Generate an easy-to-understand visual infographic summarizing the analysis and **output it as a downloadable PNG image file**. The PNG infographic should visually represent the following components (ensure private details like specific names or cities are generalized):
1.  **Coverage vs Benchmark Table:** Show Category, Your Coverage, Benchmark, and Status.
2.  **Premium Value Scorecard:** Visualize a Price vs Coverage Score (1–10) and Value vs Market Position (Below Avg / Avg / Above Avg).
3.  **Risk Heatmap:** Highlight Risk Areas, Level (Low/Med/High), and Notes.
4.  **Decision Snapshot:** Display the final recommendation (Stay / Optimize / Shop Around) and Confidence Level (High / Medium / Low).
